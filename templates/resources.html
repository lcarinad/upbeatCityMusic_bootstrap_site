Resources for Your Classroom

Resource Description
Institute for Composer Diversity Database of composers from upderrepresented groups. Composers can be found through several different search filters including gender, racial/ethnic demographics, sexual/romantic identity, residence, and various large ensemble and chamber ensemble genres.
Carnegie Hall Musical Explorers Find authentic music, and accompanying lesson plans, recordings, pronunciation tracks, teaching videos from around the world for your students performed by professional musicians from that country.
NY Philharmonic Curriculum Guides Curriculum guides from the NY Phil's Young Peoples Concerts for Schools. Find guides like 'Music as a Change Agent' and 'Coming to New York - Immigration Voices'. Find accompanying printable activities.
The Justice Choir Songbook Downloadable songbook of new protest songs from the Justice Choir,created for the issues of our times.
Social Justice Standards Teaching standards from Learning from Justice. These standards are a road map for anti-bias education for K-12 education. There are so many opportinuties to connect repertoire, and learning objectives to these standards.
Musician of the Month Project Authenitic anti-racist free music curriculum that connects the cultures of students and cultures represented in curriculums. Unit plans for all grade levels on diverse composers, performers, and ensembles. All unit plans and teaching materials are contributed by music educators from around the world and was started by Somerville (MA) Public Schools.
Timeline of African American Music Interactive timeline from Carnegie Hall which celebrates the African American musical traditions from 1600 - Present Day. Detailed view of the evolution of American American musical genres, showcasing the characteristics of each genre and style, historical and cultural context, and listening examples. This project was conducted by Dr. Portia K. Maultsby, a pioneer in the study of African American music.
Smithsonian Folkways Recordings Find lesson plans, recordings, and more from the Smithsonian Smithsonian Folkways Recordings provides a wide range of classroom and teaching resources, including educational materials, lesson plans, and audio recordings that educators can use to teach students about diverse musical and cultural traditions from around the world.
